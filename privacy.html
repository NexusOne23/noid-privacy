The following information provides a simple overview of what happens to your personal data when you visit this website. Personal data is any data that can be used to identify you personally.

Who is responsible for data collection on this website?

How do we collect your data?

Your data is collected when you provide it to us. This may be data that you enter into our waitlist form (email address and optionally your name).

4. Data Collection on this Website

Waitlist / Google Forms

When you sign up for our waitlist, we use Google Forms to collect your email address and optionally your name.

Purpose of data processing:

  • Notification about the launch of NoID Privacy PRO (Q1 2026)
  • Invitation to beta testing (January 2026)
  • Information about early-bird offers

Legal basis:

The processing is based on your consent (Art. 6 para. 1 lit. a GDPR). You can withdraw this consent at any time by sending us an email to support@noid-privacy.com.

Storage duration:

Your data will be stored until the launch of NoID Privacy PRO (expected February 2026) or until you request deletion. After the launch, the data will be deleted unless you expressly wish to continue contact.

Transfer to third parties:

Your data is collected and stored via Google Forms. Google acts as a data processor. For more information, please see Google's privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Server Log Files

The provider of the pages automatically collects and stores information in so-called server log files, which your browser automatically transmits to us. These are:

  • Browser type and browser version
  • Operating system used
  • Referrer URL
  • Hostname of the accessing computer
  • Time of the server request
  • IP address

This data is not merged with other data sources. The collection of this data is based on Art. 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR. The website operator has a legitimate interest in the technically error-free presentation and optimization of their website.
